Will vs. Trust Comparison

Which one is right for you?

The difference between a Will and a Trust is the most common question in estate planning. While both documents determine who gets your assets, they function very differently. Use this interactive guide to explore the differences.

Feature
Last Will & Testament
Living Trust
Probate Court
Required
Avoided RECOMMENDED
Will:

Your estate must go through court supervision (probate) before assets are distributed. This can take 9-18 months.

Trust:

Assets in a trust bypass probate entirely, allowing for immediate transfer to beneficiaries without court interference.

Privacy
Public Record
100% Private RECOMMENDED
Will:

Once filed, a Will becomes a public document. Anyone can read it and see who inherited what.

Trust:

Trust documents are private contracts. No one typically sees them except the beneficiaries and the trustee.

Cost
Low Now, High Later
High Now, Low Later
Will:

Cheaper to create ($300-$1,000), but probate costs can eat up 3-7% of your estate value after death.

Trust:

More expensive to set up ($1,500-$4,000), but saves your family thousands in court fees and legal costs later.

Speed
Slow (Months/Years)
Fast (Weeks) RECOMMENDED
Will:

Heirs generally cannot access funds until the court authorizes it, which often takes over a year.

Trust:

The Successor Trustee can often distribute funds immediately for funeral costs and bills.

Incapacity
None
Built-In RECOMMENDED
Will:

A Will only works after you die. It provides no protection if you become ill or incapacitated (requires a separate Power of Attorney).

Trust:

Your Successor Trustee can step in instantly to manage your finances if you become unable to do so.

Help Me Decide

1. Do you own real estate (a home) in your name?

2. Is privacy important to you (keeping inheritance amounts secret)?

3. Do you want your heirs to receive their money immediately without court delays?

4. Is your estate worth more than $166,250? (Threshold varies by state, often ~$150k)
